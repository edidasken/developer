📚 Word of the Day
Greek
ἀγάπη
agapē
Love; divine, unconditional love

This is the highest form of love in Greek, referring to the selfless, sacrificial, unconditional love that God has for humanity and that Christians are called to have for one another. Unlike romantic or familial love, agapē is a choice and an action, not merely a feeling.

Strong's G26
Hebrew
שָׁלוֹם
shalom
Peace; wholeness, completeness, welfare

More than just the absence of conflict, shalom represents complete well-being, harmony, and prosperity. It encompasses physical health, emotional wellness, right relationships with God and others, and spiritual wholeness. It's both a greeting and a blessing in Hebrew culture.

Reading Time

Overview

Ephesians is a profound letter from the Apostle Paul to the church in Ephesus, emphasizing the mystery of the Church as the body of Christ and the unity of believers in Him.

Key Themes

  • Unity in Christ: Breaking down walls between Jews and Gentiles
  • Spiritual Blessings: Chosen, adopted, redeemed, and sealed with the Holy Spirit
  • Christian Living: Walking worthy of our calling in love and truth
  • Spiritual Warfare: The armor of God and standing firm against evil

Key Verses

"For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God, not a result of works, so that no one may boast." — Ephesians 2:8-9
